# Interview Explanations

## Ops-Copilot

Short explanation:

Ops-Copilot is the flagship because it shows the full stack of what I want to be hired for: industrial workflow framing, measurable applied ML, and product packaging. I used a document-grounded assistant surface for operator support, then paired it with a SECOM-based failure-risk ranking case study so the project could be evaluated as more than a chat app.

What to emphasize:

- why the problem is ranking under limited engineering review capacity
- why PR-AUC and review-budget lift matter more than raw accuracy
- why the published model stays interpretable

## visual-qc-project

Short explanation:

Visual QC Project started as an inspection workflow demo and was upgraded into a real industrial CV case study. The important shift was adding the NEU-CLS benchmark and a low-confidence review queue so the repo demonstrates measurable defect triage, not only OpenCV usage or dashboard presentation.

What to emphasize:

- why manufacturing CV needs traceability and review, not only inference
- why a simple classical baseline is valid for a zero-cost portfolio artifact
- how the workflow and evaluation layers support each other

## smart-factory-app

Short explanation:

Smart Factory App is the predictive-maintenance support case study in the portfolio. I used the UCI AI4I dataset to turn a synthetic Industry 4.0 dashboard into a measurable maintenance prioritization system, then translated the model into a ranked queue and an illustrative cost-tradeoff story.

What to emphasize:

- why the target is imbalanced and why PR-AUC matters
- why a ranked queue is more realistic than treating every asset equally
- how the cost model helps connect ML output to maintenance decisions
